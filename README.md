# Cyclistic Rider Behavior Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes Cyclistic bike-share data to identify behavioral differences between annual members and casual riders.

The objective is to uncover actionable insights that can help Cyclistic increase annual membership conversions through data-driven marketing strategies.

The project demonstrates an end-to-end analytics workflow using Python, PostgreSQL, and Power BI.

---

## Business Problem

Cyclistic wants to convert more casual riders into annual members. To support this objective, this analysis explores how the two rider groups differ in their riding behavior and identifies opportunities for targeted marketing campaigns.

---

## Key Findings

- Annual members accounted for approximately **78%** of all rides.
- Casual riders recorded significantly longer ride durations.
- Members primarily rode during weekdays, reflecting commuter behavior.
- Casual riders were more active during weekends and afternoon leisure hours.
- Summer months presented the highest riding activity for both rider groups.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Launch targeted weekend membership campaigns.
- Promote memberships during peak casual riding hours.
- Increase marketing investment during summer.
- Highlight the long-term savings of annual memberships.
